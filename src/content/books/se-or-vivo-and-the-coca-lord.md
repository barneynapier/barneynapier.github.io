This was my first introduction to Louis de Berniers and I loved it. The humor is dry and the language is matter of fact:

_“The coffee table was held together by none other than gravity”_
_“She did not like to be vomited upon”_

And for a short book there's a lot going on. As with other books by De Berniers it can be tricky to follow all the sub-plots, but generally he does really well to intertwine them. I found the 'Gran Condomble' chapter confusing. But I think that was intentional.

I liked the magical realism too. Similar to A Hundred Years of Solitude where it all seems realistic then somebody floats off into the sky.

Few characters of note:

**Madame Rosa**

- Runs a whorehouse
- Velvet Rosa = Dionisios choice, emotionally close
- Massive brawl in her brothel later in book

**Dionisio Vivo**

- Writes the coca letters
- Philosophy lecturer
- Dead people keep being left at his door
- El brujo
- Unkillable
- Risen from the dead 2 times
- Enlarges himself when enraged (attempted mugging with Anica)
- Strong moral compass
- Tried to kill himself - has the scars

**Anica**

- loves Dionisio
- Dad supplies arms to jerarca
- She is warned to leave Dionisio by jerarca
- Is killed violently by jerarca henchman

**Jerarca**

- Fat man
- Has anica killed
- Kills lazaro in “the firedance”
- Tries to bribe Dionisio to stop writing letters - Jerez (dionisios friend) finds the money and runs off. Dionisio never realises

**The Condomble**

- How laticia aragon was born

**Las locas**

- Go to ipasueno to have a child of Dionisio
- Create a favela together on edge of town
- Dionisio later impregnates 29 of them

**Lazaro**

- Leopracy due to killing dolphins for their aphrodisiac cream
- Dies in a petrol firestorm torture

**Laticia Aragon**

- Finds Dionisio after

**Aurelio**

- Brujo of Cochadebajo de los gatos
- Gives Dionisio 2 black jaguars
- Tried to cure lazaro
