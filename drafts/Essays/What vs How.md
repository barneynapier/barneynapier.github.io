# What vs How  
  
In my experience working as a quant researcher at a hedge fund, I've realised the importance of having a separation between who chooses the goal and who gets there.  
Individuals that figure out how to solve a problem (the researchers) should not be the ones choosing which problems to solve. Obviously I'm talking about my own experience here, but often a researcher won't choose to attempt a problem that has no clear path to a solution. If I can't even fathom a method for getting to an end goal, I'm likely not going to choose that problem to solve.  
Thats not to say that I only choose problems that I can solve. More that if I have absolutely no idea where to even start, I'll probaby not choose that my main focus of effort.  
That's why its good to have a PM (or anybody) that makes the decision on which efforts are worth it and which are not. They don't consider if its possible, they just think what goals are worth attaining. It's then up to the researcher to work out how to do it.  
This is good because a lot of the time I end up finding a solution to things that I never thought would be possible. And this is as a result of my PM putting me onto problems that I wouldn't have put myself onto.
