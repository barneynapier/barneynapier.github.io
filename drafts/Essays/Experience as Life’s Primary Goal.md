# Experience as Life’s Primary Goal  
  
* Experience should be the primary goal in life  
    * Both physical and intellectual  
* Curiosity  
* "Try everything once"  
* Point  
    * Bezos regret minimisation framework  
        * Jeff Bezos founded amazon because he wanted to minimise the regret he felt later in life  
        * He worked at a hedge fund, was being paid well and generally it seemed life must have been good  
        * There was certainly a lot to lose  
        * But If he didnt try it, he would always wonder how it could have turned out, and always regret not giving it a chance  
        * Regret minimisation implies trying new things  
            * We rarely regret things that we do  
            * More often we regret what we don't do  
            * The solution being do (dont do) as much (little) as possible  
    * Explore / exploit  
        * Concept in computer science  
        * Simplified solution  
            * Use the first 1/e% of your life to explore (Call it 18-43-86)  
            * Then the rest to exploit what you enjoy  
    * Marginal returns of utility diminish with consumption  
    * Making informed choices  
        * All information is useful to us  
        * Information telling us what we dont like, or are not good at, or what hurts, is all useful  
        * Where to go and what to do are often hard questions to answer, but where not to go and what not to do are generally simpler  
* Counter  
    * I am already happy  
        * The fish in the small pond knows nothing of the ocean  
        * However, happiness in ignorance is still happiness  
        * Then stop reading and continue being happy  
    * I already know what I enjoy and want to just keep doing that  
        * You want to stop exploring and begin exploiting  
        * You may be lucky enough to find out what you enjoy early on  
        * Don't let me stop you  
        * Just consider you may love something more that you dont yet know  
        * Let me just ask: How do you know this is the thing you enjoy most in the world?  
    * I know I dont want to do something  
        * Have you done it? If not, how do you know?  
    * That new experience looks scary  
    * I'd rather buy something than do something  
    * My friend told me I wont like it  
        * This is a form of experience through others  
        * However, does your friend know you well?  
            * If yes, then you have the benefit of gaining experience vicariously through him/her  
            * If no, why should you trust their opinion  
            * Either way, your friend cannot know you as well as you know yourself. Accepting their judgement as your own involves taking responsibility for their judgement
