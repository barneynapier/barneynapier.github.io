My Research  
  
FAT  
The most obvious impact of intermittent fasting is that your body uses more fat. The process in your body of creating energy is to use the macronutrients in the following order: 1. Carbohydrates 2. Fats 3. Proteins Note that carbs are not stored on their own in your body. They are either converted into proteins or fats.  
When you do intermittent fasting, you are removing this initial source of energy (carbs), and forcing your body to convert fats and proteins into energy instead. Your body will always use up fast before proteins because that’s exactly what fat is: carbs converted into energy to be used when you need it. So fat will go next and only when your levels of fat are sufficiently low will the body begin to use proteins for energy.  
  
HUMAN GROWTH HORMONE (HGH)  
HGH is what steroids are. The base level of HGH in humans is erratically inconsistent and rather low. Intermittent fasting: • Increases the base level of secretion • Makes secretion more consistent  
[https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC329619/](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC329619/) [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1548337/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1548337/) [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3127426/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3127426/)  
But the real benefit of this is not additional HGH, it is the benefits that come from additional HGH: • Increased muscle mass • Decreased body fat • Increased energy levels.

INSULIN  
Intermittent fasting also improves your body’s insulin sensitivity, permitting homeostasis (remember that from GCSE biology?) to function more properly. This means: • Lower insulin levels • Body fat is more accessible to be used as energy  
[https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15640462/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15640462/)  
  
CELLULAR REGENRATION  
There is a special process in the cells of the body (specifically the brain -?) called autophagy, this is the process of homeostasis in your cells in which they are degraded and recycled into newer, healthier versions of themselves. Intermittent fasting induces this process of autophagy and hence is good for the cell repair and regeneration. Basically, by not eating you are allowing your body to undergo a natural process of cleansing itself of weak or diseased cells, regenerating them into new and healthier ones. This works especially well in the brain and hence is an added prevention measure for diseases like Alzheimer’s or cancer.  
[https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3106288/](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3106288/)
