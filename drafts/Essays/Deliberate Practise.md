# Deliberate Practise  
  
[FS Article](https://fs.blog/2021/04/deliberate-practice-guide/) Notes  
[FS Article](https://fs.blog/2021/04/deliberate-practice-guide/) Notes  
*   
* Deliberate practise = Practising specfic parts of a skill  
*   
* Deliberate practise isnt much fun, its hard work  
  
How  
*   
* Identify an area to improve upon  
*   
* Break it up into small chunks  
*   
* Identify objective standards for performance evaluation  
*   
* Study top performers  
*   
* Design practise activities to mimic what they do  
*   
* Get feedback on your progress  
*   
* Move onto a new area when you have improved this one enough  
  
Principles  
“No matter what the field, the most effective approach to improving performance is to follow a single set of principles.” - Anders Ericsson  
*   
* Seek out areas of weakness  
*   
* Before starting a practise session, think: (1) What am I working on (2) Why am I working on it (3) How do I intend to improve it?  
*   
* Keep in mind that most learning comes outside your comfort zone  
*   
* Go 10% harder than what you find comfortable  
*   
* Rest (sleep is great for long term memory)  
*   
* Focus: When practising, truly practise, when resting, truly rest  
*   
* Experts rarely spend more than 4-5 hours a day deliberately practising  
  
Tips  
*   
* Rest: Make a list of relaxing activities that you can do outside of practise (music, journal, walking, etc)  
*   
* Get lots of feedback  
*   
* Get a teacher, deliberate practise is much easier with a coach of some kind  
*   
* Motivate yourself intrinsically (list the long term goal and keep it in mind)  
*   
* Acknowledge this will be a life long process most likely  
*   
* Train yourself to pay attention for longer periods of time (Focus for longer)  
*   
* Be aware of what you are giving up  
  
Downsides  
*   
* You spend all your time practising and not living (make sure to maintain a balance)  
*   
* You might end up working on the wrong things  
*   
* Deliberate practise makes you focus on your weaknesses but can be demotivating  
*   
* Deliberate practise happens in the flow state, right on the edge of our ability  
*   
* Comfort zone / learning zone / panic zone
