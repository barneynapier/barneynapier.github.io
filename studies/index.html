studies/

studies & papers i've found interesting.

36 Questions

Aron's set of escalating questions designed to generate closeness between two strangers.

Self Confidence

Bandura's foundational paper introducing self-efficacy — the belief in your own ability — and arguing it governs whether you attempt something, how hard you try, and how long you persist.

Suppression of Unwanted Thoughts

Evidence that actively suppressing fearful thoughts can improve mental health rather than backfiring.

Effect of Exercise on Depression

A network meta-analysis finding exercise is an effective treatment for depression, with walking, running, and strength training among the most effective.

Deactivating Facebook & Instagram

A randomised experiment showing that deactivating Facebook and Instagram before an election increased happiness and reduced anxiety.

Illusion and Well-Being

Taylor & Brown argue that positive illusions about oneself are linked to better mental health.

Optimism Increases Longevity

Higher optimism is associated with longer lifespan and greater odds of living past 85.

A Wandering Mind is an Unhappy Mind

Killingsworth & Gilbert find people are less happy when their minds wander, regardless of what they are doing.

Repetitive Negative Thinking & Subjective Cognitive Decline

Rumination and chronic worry are associated with subjective cognitive decline in older adults.

Music Drug

Blocking opioid receptors with naltrexone dampened both the pleasure and the displeasure people felt from music — evidence that endogenous opioids underlie musical emotion, and that music draws on the same reward pathways as food, drugs, and sex.

Placebo Sleep Affects Cognitive Functioning

Simply believing you slept well improves cognitive performance, independent of actual sleep quality.

Sleep & Biological Ageing Clocks in Middle and Late Life

Links sleep patterns in middle and late life to biological measures of ageing.

The Effects of Gratitude Expression on Neural Activity

Kini et al. find a brief gratitude-letter writing intervention increases neural sensitivity to gratitude in the medial prefrontal cortex, an effect still present three months later.
